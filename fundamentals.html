Fundamentals

What this page is for

Fundamentals are non-negotiable rules everyone in IT and Digital follows. They define how we measure, collaborate, source, invest and deliver quality across the product organisation.

Use this page to understand what each fundamental requires, why it exists, and who is responsible for it.

How we provide insights in IT & Digital and outside for decision making

Delivery measurements

Why

We measure progress, cost, risk, and resources to track whether we are delivering planned outcomes on time and within budget. This helps us spot issues early, align spend with progress, manage risks, deploy the right capacity, and make timely, evidence-based decisions.

What

Actuals vs budget vs forecast, reported in absolute numbers. Overall status is evaluated by the initiative lead based on:

  • Progress: are we where we expected to be?
  • Percentage of major activities completed vs planned
  • Cost performance: will we be able to complete within budget?
  • Risk: is the risk profile understood and are risks being managed?
  • Resources: do we have the staffing needed from IT and Digital and the business?
Who Make: IT Head of Product, IT Delivery Lead, Product Owner, Product Manager
Use: IT Delivery Partner, IT Portfolio Partner, IT LT
When Q2 2026 (awaiting KPI framework)

Outcome and value measurements

Why

To be outcome obsessed and a value enabler, we need to understand which business KPIs we aim to influence, and how we influence them via leading indicators.

What

For investments above 300k EUR, define leading indicators and align them with the business owner. These should be followed up at an agreed interval (1 to 4 times per year), each with a target. We measure the number of investments compliant with this requirement and track actual vs target for each leading indicator.

Who Make: IT Value Partner, IT Head of Product, Product Manager, Product Owner
Use: Business Owner, IT Portfolio Partner, IT LT
How Leading indicators, CAPEX Insights, Supporting Material templates, leading indicators library
When Q2 2026 (awaiting KPI framework)

Operational measurements

Why

Operational measurements are essential for running our operation efficiently and ensuring our IT Products are tracking towards the right level of value adoption, availability, stability and security.

What

Daily performance is tracked across, for example:

  • Availability of services, SLAs and Business Impact Index (BII)
  • Ticket and incident resolution times and volume
  • Security KPIs, including vulnerability remediation
  • Recovery KPIs (RTO/RPO)

Product team engagement measurements

Why

We measure team engagement to understand how teams are functioning beyond delivery responsibilities. It provides insight into the parameters that shape team collaboration and individual performance, helping us address team challenges early and support the development of empowered, high-performing teams.

What

Team engagement in IT and Digital is measured on four parameters: psychological safety, team member satisfaction and engagement, collaboration, and performance and purpose.

An action plan is defined based on the results. Head of IT Product is responsible for ensuring the measurement is carried out and that follow-up actions are tracked and completed.

Who Make: Head of IT Product
Use: Head of IT Product, IT Delivery Leads, IT Product teams
How Anonymous quarterly tracking. Action plan actively carried out and revisited as part of continuous team development.

Operating the IT product and decommissioning

Why

We aim to maintain secure, stable, cost-efficient and high-quality IT products at all times, across the full lifecycle from active operation through to planned retirement.

What

Operating the product efficiently includes maintaining applications, handling business requests for minor enhancements, tracking changes, managing incidents and keeping documentation current.

Decommissioning covers the planned retirement of a service: data migration and archiving, contract and licence closure, security cleanup, infrastructure removal, data governance, CMDB updates, service agreement adjustments and hardware decommissioning.

Product roadmaps

Why

IT product teams need to manage their products across the full lifecycle. A clear outlook for future products, while building, running and retiring existing ones, strengthens the partnership between IT product teams and the business by keeping the roadmap aligned to business strategy and product vision.

What

A product roadmap is a time-sequenced, outcome-focused statement of the product's strategic direction, covering features, data, maintenance and more. It is updated regularly to reflect the IT product portfolio for the next 1 to 3 years, including future ideas, items under development, items in operation and planned decommissioning.

Who Make: Head of IT Product, Product Manager, Product Owner
Use: Head of IT Area, IT Enablement Team, IT Product Team

Planning

Why

Planning creates shared understanding, defines clear milestones, manages risks and dependencies, and enables informed prioritisation so teams can balance demand with capacity and stay responsive to change.

What
  • Run regular, time-boxed planning to prioritise work, size capacity and define clear outcomes
  • Address dependencies and track them alongside risks with clear mitigations
  • Treat the plan as a commitment to what will be done in the given period, while recognising that plans should change when important new information or priorities emerge
Who Make: IT Product Team (IT Delivery Lead, Head of IT Product)
Use: Enablement Teams, IT Product Teams, Product Owner, Product Manager
How Plans and dependencies documented and tracked in Azure DevOps. Dependencies to other product teams aligned through dialogue before reporting via Dependency Forms or directly in Azure DevOps.

Inflow of planned work

Why

By staying close to our customers and continuously translating their business strategy, objectives and KPIs into concrete IT deliverables, competency needs and capacity plans, we ensure we can deliver the right things at the right time.

What
  • Systematic intake and assessment of upcoming needs via a pipeline of ideas
  • Translation of needs into a product roadmap, competency profiles and capacity forecasts
  • Active capacity management covering both planned delivery and reserved buffer for maturation and unplanned work
Who Make: Head of IT Product, Enablement Team (for entirely new products or complex cross-functional initiatives)
Use: Enablement Team, IT Product Team, Product Owner, Product Manager
How Quarterly and/or monthly customer insight sessions. Standardised funnel process: Discover, Qualify, Funnel, then Maturation, Plan, Deliver. Captured in Azure DevOps and aligned with available funding. Roadmaps with minimum 12-month perspective.

Inflow of dynamic and emergent work

Why

By acknowledging and planning for dynamic and emergent work, we protect delivery predictability, quality and team health. We reduce disruption, make realistic commitments and maintain flow, both within and across product teams.

What
  • Proactively identify, track and plan capacity for incidents, emergent work and outages
  • Allocate a percentage of capacity based on historical data and adjust as necessary
  • Apply lightweight triage and time-boxed investigation before committing to larger deliveries
  • Proactively reduce incidents and emergent work through built-in quality, inspect and adapt, and by addressing root causes and technical debt
Who IT Product Team
How Plan with incidents and emergent work as a starting point and adjust based on data. Make dynamic work visible. Define entry and triage policies. Create follow-up tasks to reduce future incidents through automation and improvements.

Sourcing of consultants

Why

To ensure cost-efficiency, quality delivery and compliance with procurement policies.

What

External consultants fall into three categories: freelancers (cost-effective for temporary roles or staff augmentation), consultancy firms (for team-based or managed service deliveries), and niche suppliers (specialised services not covered by main suppliers).

Approved suppliers are: Emagine, IBM, Accenture and Netcompany. Any other supplier requires IT LT Director approval.

Who All Arla IT employees involved in sourcing or engaging external consultants
When Whenever external IT expertise is needed and internal resources are unavailable or unsuitable

Sourcing of software, services and hardware

Why

To ensure commercial excellence, compliance and the best possible agreements for Arla through competitive sourcing.

What

IT Procurement and VMO leads all contract and commercial discussions and ensures necessary involvement of IT Legal. Competitive processes such as RFPs, tenders and benchmarks are encouraged to explore options and strengthen negotiation positions.

Who All IT employees involved in software, hardware (not in Coupa catalogues) or IT managed services purchases. IT Procurement and VMO involvement is mandatory.
How Engage IT Procurement early. Do not initiate commercial or contractual dialogue with suppliers directly. Follow Arla's procurement principles, platform selection framework and sourcing process.
When From the moment a need for software, hardware or managed IT services is identified, before any supplier contact

Who Make: Product Manager, IT Portfolio Partner, Business Owner, IT Value Partner

Forecast: IT investments

Why

We need a clear view of expected spend to stay within allocated funding, and an estimate at completion to confirm we remain within the approved project investment budget and that the business case and value are still valid.

What

Use the Cost Estimation template to calculate the initial budget forecast and form the foundation for the budget requested through CapexInsights. For monthly follow-up, compare actuals with a best-estimate forecast of expected outcomes, not a forecast of how to spend remaining funds. Data is automatically uploaded for centralised reporting.

Who Make: Head of IT Product, Delivery Partner
Use: Head of IT Product, IT Product Enablement, Head of IT Area, CIO Office
How Cost Estimation sheet for initial budget input to CapexInsights. Standard Forecast follow-up template for monthly follow-up. Data automatically uploaded to SAP BW for centralised reporting.

How we ensure we deliver the needed quality

Service Level Agreement

Why

By understanding the business's availability and service-level needs, we use SLAs to deliver reliable, predictable application performance and consistently meet expectations.

What

An SLA is a contract between Arla and the business (and, where needed, a supplier) that defines expected service levels. In Arla IT, we use two types: Component SLAs and Shared SLAs. Both product teams and suppliers are measured. SLAs track and report performance to ensure alignment, validate agreed service levels and confirm value for money.

Who Head of IT Product, Vendor Management
How ServiceNow, Business Agreement Template
From 1 January 2026

Business application criticality

Why

Setting a business criticality level for all applications defines the resilience requirements and enables faster recovery. Each criticality level carries specific requirements for support level, solution resilience and security.

What

Business criticality is a joint assessment between IT and the business that must be completed each time a new application is added to the product, including a Business Impact Assessment (BIA) where relevant. The criticality level must be kept current throughout the product lifecycle.

Who Make: Head of IT Product, Product Owner
Use: Delivery Lead, Head of Product, IT Architecture Partner
How CMDB (master system for criticality), LeanIX, ServiceNow, Business Case
From January 2026

Built-in quality

Why

We build quality in from the start to deliver agreed outcomes at the right speed. By balancing fit-for-purpose quality practices with efficient execution, we reduce rework, accelerate delivery and sustain long-term value, ensuring we meet business needs without compromising reliability, security or compliance.

What

A mindset and set of practices that integrate quality throughout the development process. This includes security baselines, Information Security Policy, coding guidelines, documentation standards, test principles, SAP guidelines, architectural guiding principles, sustainability principles, data governance, and release and deployment guidelines, all scaled according to agreed business criticality.

Who IT Product teams
How IT Product Teams select and adapt practices and supporting materials in line with the product's business criticality.
