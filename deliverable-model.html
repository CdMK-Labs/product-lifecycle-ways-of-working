Deliverable Model

What this page is for

This page explains the deliverables that support our product lifecycle model in practice.

The purpose is to make it easier for product teams to document what is needed to move through the lifecycle with clarity, while staying aligned with our financial model, governance processes and fundamentals.

The model is designed to support decisions, delivery and operations. It is not meant to add documentation for its own sake. It should help teams create the right level of structure for the stage they are in, using a small set of reusable deliverables rather than a large number of disconnected documents.

Principles

  • Document to support decisions, delivery and operations, not for its own sake
  • Keep deliverables as light as possible for the stage
  • Prefer updating existing deliverables over creating new ones
  • Use one source of truth where possible
  • Let the stage exit criteria define the minimum documentation needed
  • Treat deliverables as information objects, not fixed file types
  • Keep the lifecycle model and financial model aligned

How the model aligns

The deliverable model follows the same logic as our lifecycle and aligns it with the financial model. This creates one shared structure from early shaping to live product operations.

Early Shaping

Covers Value Proposition and Product Discovery.

The purpose is to clarify the opportunity, reduce uncertainty and decide whether the topic should move forward.

Development

Covers Align & Plan.

The purpose is to shape the commitment, bring together value case and cost case, and secure the required approval.

Execution

Covers Develop & Deliver and Deploy.

The purpose is to turn the approved scope into a live, usable and supportable change.

Operations

Covers Operate & Monitor, Measure & Learn and Adapt & Optimize.

The purpose is to run the product, learn from live use and improve it over time.

Core deliverables

The model uses a small set of core deliverables that can be reused across the lifecycle.

Early Shaping

Opportunity Brief

A lightweight deliverable that explains why the topic matters, what value it could create and whether it should move forward.

Discovery Pack

A lightweight deliverable that captures what was learned, which options were explored, what risks and dependencies matter, and whether the topic is ready for planning and approval.

Development

Business Case & Delivery Decision Pack

The main decision deliverable for approval and commitment.

It is supported by four appendices:

  • KPI Appendix
  • Architecture Appendix
  • Estimates & Financial Appendix
  • Risk Appendix

Execution

Delivery Pack

The practical execution deliverable used to track scope, progress, dependencies, quality and readiness for release.

Release & Readiness Pack

The deliverable used to confirm safe release, rollout, support readiness and go-live control.

Operations

Product Review Pack

The recurring live-product review deliverable used to support ongoing operations, learning and improvement.

It includes three standing sections:

  • Operational Health
  • Measurement & Learning
  • Optimization & Lifecycle Decisions

How to use this model

Start with the lifecycle stage you are in. Use the relevant deliverable as the minimum documentation needed to support that stage.

Do not create more material than the stage requires. Update existing deliverables where possible, and link to source tools, dashboards or working notes instead of duplicating information.

The aim is to make decisions, delivery and live product management clearer. The model should help teams create enough structure to work well, while keeping the documentation practical and aligned with both the lifecycle and the financial model.
