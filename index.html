Product Lifecycle

This site is the practical reference for how we work across the product lifecycle. It translates our model into something teams can use in practice: what each stage is for, when to enter it, what to deliver, who to involve, and which templates, processes and standards to work with.

The lifecycle is built around full product responsibility. Product teams do not just hand work over from planning to delivery to operations. They carry the product forward across the lifecycle and improve it over time. That is how we create stronger ownership, better flow and more consistent value delivery, while staying within our fundamentals and balancing speed, stability and compliance.

Stages

01

Value Proposition

Start with the why. Clarify the problem, the value to create and the strategic fit.

 02

Product Discovery

Understand before we build. Explore options, involve the right people and reduce uncertainty early.

 03

Align & Plan

Shape the work for commitment. Clarify scope, team, dependencies, cost and the business case.

 04

Develop & Deliver

Build in small, quality steps. Make work visible, document as you go and collaborate across products.

 05

Deploy

Release and roll out safely. Ensure operational readiness, prepare users and support adoption at go-live.

 06

Operate & Monitor

Keep the product healthy. Track service health, respond to incidents and maintain control in live use.

 07

Measure & Learn

Understand adoption and outcomes. Use data and user insight to see what is working and what needs attention.

 08

Adapt & Optimize

Turn learning into action. Improve value, reliability and technical health, and retire what no longer serves.

How to use this site

Use the lifecycle model as your entry point. Click a stage to see what good looks like in practice: entry criteria, key deliverables, involved roles, templates, governance touchpoints and relevant supporting processes. Start with the stage you are in now, but use the full model to look ahead and work with lifecycle thinking rather than isolated delivery steps. This site is intended as a practical guide, not a heavy process layer. It should help teams move with clarity while staying aligned on fundamentals.
