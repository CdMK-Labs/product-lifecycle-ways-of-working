## What this stage is for

Measure & Learn is where we step back from day-to-day operations and assess how the product is performing from a usage, adoption and outcome perspective. The purpose is to understand whether the product is being used as intended, whether users are getting value from it, and whether the expected business outcomes are developing in the right direction.

This stage runs in parallel with Operate & Monitor, but it has a different focus. Operate & Monitor is about service health, incidents and operational control. Measure & Learn is about usage patterns, adoption, user behaviour, sentiment and outcome signals.

IT & Digital provides data, measurements and insight, while partnering with the business to interpret what the signals mean. Accountability for business outcomes sits with the business. The product team's role is to make signals visible, usable and part of an ongoing dialogue.

The insight generated here feeds directly into Adapt & Optimize.

## Principles

- Measure to learn and act, not just to report
- Prioritise meaningful signals over easy-to-measure ones
- IT provides insight; the business interprets value
- Turn measurements into decisions, not dashboards

## Entry criteria

- The product is live and in active use
- There is enough usage, adoption or outcome data to review
- Relevant stakeholders are available to interpret what the signals mean

## Key activities and responsibilities

- Track usage and adoption (PM)
- Analyse user behaviour and friction (AS)
- Gather feedback with business (BO)
- Translate insights into priorities (PO)
- Align on what signals mean (HoP)

## Common pitfalls

**Confusing this stage with Operate & Monitor**

Operate & Monitor focuses on service health and operational control. Measure & Learn focuses on usage, adoption and outcome signals. They answer different questions.

**Tracking only what is easy to measure**

Easy metrics are not always meaningful. The point is to create useful insight, not just data volume.

**Treating IT & Digital as owner of business value**

IT & Digital provides data and insight, but accountability for value realisation sits with the business.

**Collecting data without interpretation**

Measures only become useful when they are discussed, understood and linked to action.

**Letting learning stop at reporting**

The purpose of this stage is to inform improvement through Adapt & Optimize, not just create dashboards.
