## What this process is for

Report Financial Status brings together the financial picture for active investments and live products so that leaders can see where things stand, spot issues and decide what needs attention.

This is not about producing reports for their own sake. It is about making the financial health of the portfolio visible so decisions happen at the right time.

## When to use it

- At regular portfolio review points, typically monthly
- When a significant financial variance has been escalated
- When leadership needs an updated view of investment performance
- When products in live operation need a cost and value health check
- When financial inputs are needed for planning or prioritisation cycles

This process runs across all financial stages but is most visible during Execution and Operation and Evaluation. It connects to portfolio governance and leadership review rhythms.

## Key questions to answer

- What is the overall financial health of active investments?
- Which investments are on track, which are at risk and which need intervention?
- Are live products delivering value proportionate to their cost?
- Where should we redirect attention, funding or support?
- Are there patterns across the portfolio that need a systemic response?

## Inputs

- Updated forecasts from active investments
- Actuals and variance analysis
- Delivery status and milestone progress
- Live product cost and value metrics
- Escalations from the Forecast Financials process

## Activities

- Consolidate financial status across active investments and live products
- Highlight material variances, risks and opportunities
- Prepare a clear, scannable summary for leadership and portfolio review
- Connect financial status to delivery and product health
- Recommend follow-up actions where needed

## Outputs and decisions

### Confirm status
- Financial position is healthy and no action is needed
- Continue with current approach

### Flag issue
- A specific investment or product has a financial concern that needs visibility
- Issue is documented and assigned for follow-up

### Trigger follow-up
- Financial status requires a deeper review, a reprioritisation conversation or a replan
- Follow-up action is initiated with clear ownership and timeline

## Who is involved

### Portfolio Partner
- leads the consolidation and presentation of financial status across the portfolio

### Head of Product
- provides product-level context and supports interpretation of financial signals

### Delivery Lead
- contributes delivery-level financial detail and flags issues from the ground

**Others who may contribute:** Finance Business Partner, Business Owner, IT Value Partner.

## Common pitfalls

**Reporting without recommending**

A status report that lists numbers without highlighting what needs attention is not useful. Always include a clear view of what is on track, what is not and what should happen next.

**Waiting for perfect data**

Financial reporting works best when it is timely, not when it is perfect. A good-enough view shared at the right time is more valuable than a precise report that arrives too late.

**Keeping financial status separate from delivery and product health**

If financial reports live in isolation from delivery progress and product outcomes, decision makers get a fragmented picture. Connect the financial view to the work.

**Over-reporting detail, under-reporting insight**

Leaders need patterns, exceptions and recommendations. They do not need line-by-line cost breakdowns in a portfolio review. Match the level of detail to the audience.
