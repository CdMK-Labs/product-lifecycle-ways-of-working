## What this stage is for

Develop & Deliver is where the product team turns approved scope into working product outcomes. The purpose is to build, configure, integrate or implement the agreed changes step by step, in a way that keeps quality visible and prepares the work for safe release.

This stage covers different delivery types: packaged solutions, SaaS changes and tailored development. The exact way of working may vary by context, but the discipline stays the same: deliver in visible increments, build quality in as you go, and stay aligned with dependencies and approved intent.

The stage ends when the relevant deliverables are ready to move into Deploy.

## Principles

- Deliver in small, visible increments rather than large batches
- Build quality, security and documentation in from the start, not at the end
- Adjust as you learn, while staying within approved scope and intent
- Collaborate actively with teams you depend on

## Entry criteria

- There is a clear and actionable plan for execution
- Scope and backlog items are ready enough to start
- Key dependencies, risks and constraints are visible
- The required people, environments, tools and funding are in place

## Key activities and responsibilities

- Build, configure, integrate or implement the agreed changes in manageable increments
- Validate outputs continuously through review, feedback, testing, demos or other quality checks
- Document decisions, changes and operationally relevant information as the work progresses
- Build security, service quality and other non-functional requirements into the work
- Refine backlog, priorities or sequencing as learning emerges; escalate material changes back to Align & Plan

## Common pitfalls

**Leaving quality and documentation until the end**

Quality, documentation and operational readiness should be built in as the work progresses, not as a final sprint.

**Delivering too much at once**

Large batches reduce visibility and make validation, learning and coordination harder.

**Working in isolation from dependencies**

Delivery often slows when teams do not actively follow up with other products, platforms or enabling teams.

**Silently absorbing material changes to scope**

If scope, risk or dependencies shift materially, loop back to Align & Plan. Do not absorb the change without realignment.

**Blurring delivery with deployment**

The purpose of this stage is to get changes ready to deploy. The actual release and rollout happen in Deploy.
