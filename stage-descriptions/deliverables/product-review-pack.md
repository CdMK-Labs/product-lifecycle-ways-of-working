## What this deliverable is for

The Product Review Pack is the recurring deliverable used to support live product review across operations, learning and improvement.

Its purpose is to bring the most important live-product signals together in one place: how the product is performing in operation, how it is being used, what we are learning from adoption and outcome signals, and which improvement or lifecycle decisions should follow.

The Product Review Pack is not only a reporting artifact. It is a decision and alignment deliverable. It should help the product team and business stakeholders review the live product with the right balance of operational control, product insight and forward-looking action.

The pack should make it easier to connect day-to-day product reality with product priorities, improvement decisions and longer-term lifecycle thinking.

## When to use it

Use this deliverable in Operations.

It should be used when:
- the product is live and in active use
- the team needs a recurring view of service health, adoption, usage and improvement needs
- product and business stakeholders need a shared basis for review and decision-making
- the team wants to turn live-product insight into clear next actions

The Product Review Pack supports the three operational lifecycle stages together:
- [Operate & Monitor](stage.html?s=operate-and-monitor)
- [Measure & Learn](stage.html?s=measure-and-learn)
- [Adapt & Optimize](stage.html?s=adapt-and-optimize)

It should normally be used on a recurring cadence, for example monthly, quarterly, or another rhythm that fits the product and governance context.

## Who is typically involved

### Head of Product
Usually co-owns the Product Review Pack and helps ensure the review balances live stability, product priorities and long-term sustainability.

### Product Owner
Usually co-owns the Product Review Pack and helps translate live insight into product priorities, decisions and next actions.

### Product Manager
Contributes business and portfolio perspective, especially where priorities, value, investment direction or larger trade-offs are involved.

### IT Delivery Lead
Brings visibility to service health, delivery-related follow-up, recurring issues and operational discipline.

### Adoption Specialist
Helps bring user behavior, adoption, sentiment and friction into the review.

### Product Architect
Contributes architectural, technical debt, resilience and simplification perspective where relevant.

### Business Owner
Contributes interpretation of business outcomes, value signals and business-side priorities or trade-offs.

**Others who may contribute:** IT Specialists / Developers, support roles, super users, business stakeholders, vendors where relevant.

## Minimum contents

The Product Review Pack should bring together the most important live-product signals and decisions in a way that is easy to review regularly. It should stay structured and decision-oriented, not turn into a large reporting pack.

### 1. Product summary
A short summary of the product, the review scope and the most important messages for the review period.

This section should make it easy to understand the context quickly.

### 2. Review period
A clear indication of which period the review covers.

This helps make trends, actions and follow-up easier to track over time.

### 3. Operational Health
A summary of the most important service health and operational signals.

This should typically include:
- service overview
- service levels or operational commitments
- incidents and trends
- recurring issues or workarounds
- monitoring and alert signals
- maintenance actions
- operational risks

This section should make the live condition of the product visible without becoming a detailed incident report.

### 4. Measurement & Learning
A summary of the most important usage, adoption and outcome signals.

This should typically include:
- leading KPIs
- lagging KPIs
- adoption metrics
- usage patterns
- friction or drop-off points
- user feedback and sentiment
- benefit or outcome signals
- key learnings

This section should help the team and the business discuss what the signals mean, not only display numbers.

### 5. Optimization & Lifecycle Decisions
A summary of the most important improvement needs and lifecycle choices.

This should typically include:
- prioritized improvement opportunities
- technical debt items
- reliability or resilience actions
- simplification opportunities
- retirement candidates
- recommendation on next path where relevant

This section should turn insight into product decisions.

### 6. Key decisions
A short summary of the main decisions made in the review.

This should make it easy to see what was agreed, challenged or escalated.

### 7. Actions, owners and dates
A clear view of what happens next, who owns it and by when.

This section is important to ensure the review leads to action rather than passive reporting.

### 8. Owner and contributors
The people responsible for the pack and the main contributors involved.

## What good looks like

A good Product Review Pack is regular, clear and action-oriented.

It should:
- make the current live condition of the product visible
- distinguish clearly between operational health, product learning and improvement decisions
- help the team and business stakeholders discuss what the signals mean
- make recurring issues, weak adoption or growing complexity visible rather than normalised
- support deliberate prioritisation across new value, reliability, debt reduction and simplification
- lead to clear actions, owners and follow-up

A strong Product Review Pack helps the product team stay in control of the live product and avoid separating run, learning and improvement into disconnected conversations.

## Common pitfalls

**Turning the pack into passive reporting**

If the pack only reports status and does not support decisions or next actions, it loses much of its value.

**Mixing operational health and product learning without distinction**

Service health, adoption and lifecycle improvement are connected, but they are not the same discussion.

**Drowning the review in too much detail**

The pack should surface the signals that matter most, not reproduce every dashboard or ticket list.

**Ignoring weak signals because the product is live**

Poor adoption, recurring friction, manual workarounds or growing technical debt are important signals and should not be treated as background noise.

**Using the review only for short-term firefighting**

The pack should support immediate actions where needed, but also help the team make better medium- and long-term decisions.

**Leaving actions unclear**

A review without ownership and follow-up dates rarely changes much.

## Related lifecycle stages

This deliverable is most closely linked to the three lifecycle stages that run in Operations:

- [Operate & Monitor](stage.html?s=operate-and-monitor), which focuses on service health, incidents, performance and operational control
- [Measure & Learn](stage.html?s=measure-and-learn), which focuses on usage, adoption, feedback and outcome signals
- [Adapt & Optimize](stage.html?s=adapt-and-optimize), which focuses on turning insight into improvement, simplification and lifecycle decisions

It can also feed back into earlier stages of the lifecycle.

For example, the Product Review Pack may lead to:
- normal product improvements
- a larger topic entering [Value Proposition](stage.html?s=value-proposition)
- deeper investigation through [Product Discovery](stage.html?s=product-discovery)
- a more structured case entering [Align & Plan](stage.html?s=align-and-plan)
- retirement or decommissioning decisions over time

In that sense, the Product Review Pack is both an operational review deliverable and a mechanism for feeding live-product learning back into the lifecycle.

## Templates and supporting materials

The core template for this deliverable is the [Product Review Pack template](supporting-materials/product-review-pack/).

Supporting materials may include:
- service dashboards
- incident summaries
- adoption or usage dashboards
- KPI views
- feedback summaries
- technical debt logs
- improvement backlogs
- retirement candidate lists
- action logs from prior reviews

Where possible, the pack should link to source material rather than duplicate detailed operational or analytical data.

The aim is not to create a large reporting deck. It is to create one recurring review deliverable that helps the team and stakeholders stay aligned on product health, learning and action.
