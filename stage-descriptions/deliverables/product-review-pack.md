## What this pack is for

The Product Review Pack is the recurring deliverable used to support live product review across operations, learning and improvement. It brings the most important live product signals together in one place: how the product is performing, how it is being used, what we are learning, and which improvement or lifecycle decisions should follow.

It is not only a reporting artifact. It should help the product team and business stakeholders review the live product together and turn insight into action.

## What's in this pack

- **01-Product-Review-Pack.docx** main pack. Three standing sections: **Operational Health** (service health and operational signals), **Measurement & Learning** (usage, adoption and outcome signals) and **Optimization & Lifecycle Decisions** (improvement needs and product choices). Every review ends with agreed decisions and actions with owners and dates.

## When to use this pack

Use this pack during Operation & Evaluation: [Operate & Monitor](../../stage.html?s=operate-and-monitor), [Measure & Learn](../../stage.html?s=measure-and-learn) and [Adapt & Optimize](../../stage.html?s=adapt-and-optimize).

The trigger is typically:
- the product is live and in active use
- the team needs a recurring view of service health, adoption and improvement needs
- product and business stakeholders need a shared basis for review and decision making
- the team wants to turn live product insight into clear next actions

Use the pack on a regular cadence: monthly, quarterly or another rhythm that fits the product and governance context.

A short operational health summary, a brief view of the most important usage or adoption signals, and a list of prioritised improvement actions with owners is enough to run a useful review.

## Who is typically involved

### Head of Product
- usually co-owns the pack and helps balance live stability, product priorities and long term sustainability

### Product Owner
- usually co-owns the pack and translates live insight into product priorities, decisions and next actions

### Product Manager
- contributes business and portfolio perspective, especially where investment direction or larger trade offs are involved

### IT Delivery Lead
- brings visibility to service health, delivery related follow up and recurring issues

### Adoption Specialist
- helps bring user behavior, adoption, sentiment and friction into the review

### Product Architect
- contributes architectural, technical debt, resilience and simplification perspective

### Business Owner
- contributes interpretation of business outcomes and business side trade offs

**Others who may contribute:** IT Specialists / Developers, support roles, super users, business stakeholders, vendors where relevant.

## Common pitfalls

**Turning the pack into passive reporting**

If the pack only reports status and does not support decisions or next actions, it loses most of its value.

**Mixing operational health and product learning without distinction**

Service health, adoption and lifecycle improvement are connected, but they are not the same discussion.

**Drowning the review in too much detail**

The pack should surface the signals that matter most, not reproduce every dashboard or ticket list.

**Ignoring weak signals because the product is live**

Poor adoption, recurring friction, manual workarounds or growing technical debt are important signals and should not be treated as background noise.

**Leaving actions unclear**

A review without ownership and follow up dates rarely changes much.
