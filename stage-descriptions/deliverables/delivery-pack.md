## What this pack is for

The Delivery Pack is the working deliverable used to keep execution visible and manageable once a topic has been approved and is ready to move into implementation.

It brings the most important execution information together in one place: what is being delivered, how the work is progressing, which dependencies and issues matter, what has been validated, and what needs to be resolved before deployment.

Not a status report. Keep it close to delivery tools and update it as work progresses.

## What's in this pack

- **01-Delivery-Pack.docx** main pack. Covers current scope and priorities, work breakdown, dependencies and issues, key implementation decisions, quality and test evidence, security and compliance checks, validation outcomes, and open items before deployment.

## When to use this pack

Use this pack during [Develop & Deliver](../../stage.html?s=develop-and-deliver), once the required approvals are in place and the team is moving from planning into execution.

The trigger is typically:
- the required approvals and commitments are in place
- the team is moving from planning into execution
- scope, priorities and dependencies need to stay visible during the work
- quality and operational readiness need to be tracked as part of delivery

For larger initiatives, the pack may support milestone reviews, governance follow up and dependency coordination. For smaller changes, it can be used in a lighter way to make execution visible and disciplined without unnecessary overhead.

A current scope view, a short dependency and issues summary, and a clear view of what still needs to be done before deployment is enough to keep execution visible.

## Who is typically involved

### IT Delivery Lead
- usually co-owns the pack and helps keep progress, dependencies, issues and readiness visible throughout execution

### Product Owner
- usually co-owns the pack and keeps scope, priorities and day to day product decisions aligned with the approved intent

### Head of Product
- ensures execution stays aligned with product outcomes, lifecycle responsibility and responsible trade offs

### IT Specialists / Developers
- contribute the practical execution view, implementation details, validation evidence and technical notes

### Product Architect
- ensures technical integrity, non functional quality and architectural considerations are handled during execution

**Others who may contribute:** Adoption Specialist, business stakeholders, vendors, support roles, dependent teams.

## Common pitfalls

**Treating the pack as a second project plan**

The Delivery Pack should support execution, not recreate all planning material in another format.

**Letting the pack drift away from the real work**

If it is not kept close to delivery tools, decisions and evidence, it becomes stale quickly.

**Focusing only on progress and not on readiness**

Execution is not only about getting work done. It is also about building toward a safe and usable deployment.

**Leaving quality, security and documentation until late**

These should be built into the work as it progresses, not treated as end stage tasks.

**Hiding changes in scope or assumptions**

If material changes emerge during execution, they should be made visible and handled responsibly.
