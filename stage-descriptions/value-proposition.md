## What this stage is for

The Value Proposition stage is where we clarify why something is worth attention before investing more time and effort. It frames the business need, the expected value and the strategic relevance of the opportunity at a high level.

This stage applies to new capabilities and to further investment in existing products. For new opportunities, the purpose is to decide whether the idea is strong enough to take forward. For existing products, the purpose is to clarify why further investment matters now, whether for business value, risk reduction, compliance, technical sustainability or lifecycle needs.

The output is a clear, high-level input to business prioritisation and a next step decision. That decision may point to normal product flow within an existing team, to an existing investment frame, or to a new investment path requiring further shaping and approval.

## Principles

- Start with the problem, not the solution
- Keep it high-level enough to support a conversation, not so detailed it becomes a business case
- Involve the right business and product people from the start
- Think across the full lifecycle, not only about new features or growth
- Be honest about whether the idea is strong enough to move forward

## Entry criteria

- A problem, need, risk or opportunity has been identified
- There is an accountable owner for taking it forward
- There is an initial indication that the topic may create value, reduce risk or improve sustainability

## Key activities and responsibilities

- Describe the problem or opportunity clearly (PM)
- Clarify why it matters now and the potential value (BO)
- Assess if it fits an existing product flow or investment path (HoP)
- Recommend next step: move forward, hold, reshape or stop (PM)

## Common pitfalls

**Jumping too quickly to solution ideas**

This stage is about clarifying the problem, value and strategic relevance, not designing the answer too early.

**Treating every request as equally important**

Not every idea should become an investment candidate. This stage should help filter and focus.

**Overweighting business growth and ignoring product health**

Investment can be justified by business value, but also by risk reduction, security, compliance, resilience or technical debt reduction.

**Starting with too much documentation**

If the stage becomes a full business case exercise, it slows learning and creates false precision too early.

**Forgetting lifecycle implications**

Investment needs are not only about building something new. They may also come from the need to sustain, simplify, secure or retire parts of the product landscape.
