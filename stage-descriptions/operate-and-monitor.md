## What this stage is for

Operate & Monitor is where the product is actively run in live use. The purpose is to keep the product stable, secure, available and well-performing day to day, and to maintain clear visibility into how it is behaving in production.

This stage is about operational control: service health, incidents, alerts, service levels and the follow-up needed to keep the product healthy and usable. Operations are not a handover to somewhere else. The product team stays responsible for how the product performs in production and for responding when signals show that action is needed.

Operational insight from this stage feeds directly into Measure & Learn and Adapt & Optimize. Incidents, recurring issues and service level trends should be made visible and acted on, not absorbed silently.

This stage is continuous. It runs as long as the product is active.

## Principles

- Operations are part of product ownership, not a handover
- Make operational signals visible before they become incidents
- Keep operational knowledge in systems and documentation, not in people's heads
- Connect operational insight to learning and improvement

## Entry criteria

- The release is live and stable enough for ongoing operation
- Monitoring, logging and alerting are active
- The team has visibility into product health, performance and issues
- Support and operational ownership are in place

## Key activities and responsibilities

- Monitor service health, availability, performance and operational signals
- Handle incidents, operational issues and service requests
- Track and review service levels and other operational commitments
- Perform maintenance activities and smaller upgrades as needed
- Make recurring issues and operational patterns visible for Measure & Learn and Adapt & Optimize

## Common pitfalls

**Treating operations as a handover**

In the product lifecycle model, live operation remains part of product ownership. The product team stays responsible.

**Only reacting to incidents**

Good operation is not only about fixing what breaks. It requires active monitoring and follow-up on patterns and weak signals before they become incidents.

**Keeping operational knowledge in people's heads**

Runbooks, support notes and practical documentation matter, especially when issues arise under pressure.

**Failing to connect operations with learning and improvement**

Operational insight should feed Measure & Learn and Adapt & Optimize, not remain trapped inside incident handling.
