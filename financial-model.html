Financial Model

What this page is for

The Financial Model helps teams understand how financial decisions connect to the product lifecycle. It covers five financial stages and four core financial processes that run alongside the lifecycle stages.

Use it to find the right financial conversation for your stage of work and to understand what is expected at each point.

Financial stages

01

Idea Generation

Early financial framing. Enough clarity to decide whether to explore further.

02

Exploration

Lightweight spend within a funding frame. Enough room to discover and shape.

03

Development

Investment commitment. The business case is tested and a budget is approved.

04

Execution

Active cost tracking. Forecast against approved budget as delivery progresses.

05

Operation & Evaluation

Ongoing cost and value review. Confirm the product delivers what was expected.

Core financial processes

01

Allocate Funding Frames

Set the financial boundaries for product work before detailed planning begins.

 02

Approve Investments

Decide whether a shaped business case should proceed, be reshaped, held or stopped.

 03

Forecast Financials

Keep the financial outlook for active work up to date throughout delivery and operation.

 04

Report Financial Status

Make the financial health of the portfolio visible so decisions happen at the right time.

How to use this model

Start with the financial stage that matches where your work is now. Use it to find the relevant financial process and understand what is expected.

  • Use the financial stages to understand what kind of financial conversation you are in
  • Open a process page to see what inputs, activities and decisions are involved
  • Use the matrix below to connect financial processes to lifecycle stages and deliverables
  • Keep financial conversations proportionate to the stage of work

Principles

  • Financial discipline supports delivery. It should not slow it down or exist for its own sake.
  • Keep financial conversations proportionate to the size and stage of the investment
  • Make cost and value visible together, not in separate conversations
  • Surface issues early. Small variances managed early are better than big surprises later.
  • Keep the financial model and the lifecycle model aligned
